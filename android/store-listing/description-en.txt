CircleOne -- The keyboard that writes every language.

CircleOne is a free, open-source keyboard featuring isiBheqe soHlamvu -- a featural syllabary that encodes how sounds are physically produced. Unlike the Latin alphabet, isiBheqe maps articulatory features directly: vowels are directional triangles, consonant strokes overlay them. Any human language can be written with it.

Built from Africa. Belonging to everyone.


WHAT IS isiBheqe soHlamvu?

A writing system where the shape of each character tells you how the sound is produced. Consonant strokes indicate place and manner of articulation. Vowel shapes represent tongue position and lip rounding. One script for all languages -- because human speech is universal.


KEY FEATURES

isiBheqe Syllabary Input
Type Latin transliteration (e.g., "ba", "ngu", "kwe") and see isiBheqe glyphs rendered in real time. 471 glyphs covering all consonant-vowel combinations including clicks, prenasalised, and labialized consonants.

Click Consonant Support
Long-press c, q, or x to access a full popup of click variants: aspirated (ch, qh, xh), voiced (gc, gq, gx), nasalised (nc, nq, nx), and nasal-aspirated combinations.

Word Prediction and Autocorrect
Built-in dictionaries with prediction that understands noun class prefixes, verb extensions, and common agglutinative patterns.

Universal Language Support
isiBheqe encodes articulatory features, not language-specific orthography. Zulu, Xhosa, Swahili, Shona, Mandarin, Russian, Arabic -- if it has sounds, it can be written.

Open Source and Transparent
Built on HeliBoard. Full source code available at github.com/bhengubv/circleone.


PRIVACY -- YOUR WORDS STAY ON YOUR DEVICE

CircleOne does not transmit your keystrokes anywhere. No analytics, no telemetry, no tracking, no ads. Your typing data never leaves your device.

- No keystroke logging or transmission
- No analytics or crash reporting
- No advertising of any kind
- No network requests from the keyboard
- No account required
